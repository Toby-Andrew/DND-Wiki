Brenway is a smaller city located to the south east of the country focused primarily on a mages college. Most of the small population are scholars and academics.

#### Infrastructure
The city is built around the college in its centre a large square building, the rest of the city follows suit in its shape having the outer walls be square as well. The city is split up into quadrants, one in each corner. The north west and south east corners are residential areas for the cities inhabitants. The south west corner has the cities market and shops. Finally the north east is reserved for temples and churches of various religions. Three roads lead in and out of Brenway, to the north, south and west. 

Much like the other two cities in the [[Tresden Empire]] it is quite isolated, however Brenway is perhaps the most isolated. Having been built behind a ridge of small mountains and only accessible by a winding road. This isolation was by design, to allow the mages a sanctuary to practice without much interference from travellers and other visitors. Its proximity to the desert in the south east is also of great benefit, allowing a baron space to practice the more destructive magics.

#### Architecture 
Given its location in relation to the desert its larger building are built with thin stone including the temples, library and college. Most other buildings including residential and shops are made from a high quality wood.

The surrounding wall is made of thick stone and is enchanted with runes carved in to also allow magical as well as physical protection to the city. Breaking through via pure force is impossible and It would take intensely powerful magic to get through.

The roads in a majority of the city are left as dry ground footpaths the only area with any man made stone road is the temple quadrant. This was done purposefully to allow a connection the land around them, a core philosophy within the college.

#### [[The Great Burn]]
Being as close to the desert as Brenway is meant it was hit by the brunt of the heat sooner, the mages were able to hold off the heat to some extent but it very quickly rose to be dangerous even with the protection of their magic. What little greenery there was lit ablaze spreading to many houses, but it took everything the city had to keep it contained. An hour into the great burn the wall was blasted through by the very first time in the cities existence by [[The Royal Incinerator]].

The Incinerator intended to kill and burn as many people and buildings as it could in an attempt to lure the mercenaries it was tasked with hunting into a fight, it effortlessly wiped through the cities strongest mages and waited in the burning city. Soon the mercenaries arrived and defeated the Incinerator for the last time before moving helping put out the raging fires and moving on to halt the great burn.

In the aftermath a majority of the housing was left as smoking wrecks, the wall broken, the founding mages killed and the heat unbearable. The few survivors of the Royal Incinerators attack moved to neighbouring countries, most students and professors of the college made the move to the [[Kingdom of Ostad]] to continue their research.
