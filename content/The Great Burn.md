#Event #Tresden

The disaster that brought [[Tresden Empire]] to its collapse.

The events that lead to The Great Burn started months prior. The [[Eternal Flame Cult]]s activities ramped up quickly, kidnappings and murders for their rituals, this prompted the king to launch a swift response. He hired a group of five trusted and experienced mercenaries to dismantle the cults operations.

After a couple months these mercenaries were able to track down what they thought was the head of the operations in a camp hidden within a crevice between mountains, interrupting their ritual and killing the priest. With this, cult activity halted completely for about a month, suspiciously so. This peace was abruptly broken when [[Garilant]] was attacked by [[The Royal Incinerator]] who's goal was to hunt down and kill the mercenaries.

Following this event it was discovered the [[Eternal Flame Cult]] was planning something bigger. They had used the downtime to mine up as much [[Uthalt]] as they could to create [[The Royal Incinerator]] by blackmailing an automaton inventor on a research excursion from the [[Kingdom of Ostad]].

However the Incinerator was a distraction that allowed the cult to complete their final ritual, inciting an extreme heatwave across the country and empowering the cultists. The heat was natural more intense towards the east of the [[Tresden Empire]] in the desert and as a result the old mining settlement of [[Senzad]] was wiped from the land entirely. 

In the other cities fires broke out caused by the weather or intentionally lit by cultists most prominent in [[Brenway]], where a trap was laid by [[The Royal Incinerator]] to draw the mercenaries to it, this ultimately failed and the incinerator was defeated for the last time.

In [[Garilant]], the cultists forces were the strongest in an attempt to claim the city. Eventually after an arduous fight through the city towards the castle the mercenaries faced the true leader of the [[Eternal Flame Cult]], A Tiefling sorcerer, named Brenger Vlam. Brenger was once a royal mage and trusted adviser to the King Norville, however he was driven down the path of obsession of the power held in flame. Brenger had murdered the king and all other inhabitants in the castle.

Brenger was defeated by the mercenaries and the brazier maintaining [[The Royal Incinerator]] was quenched putting a stop to the rising heat. 

The heat never fell.

With no governing body, critical damage to infrastructure and the seemingly permanent change to the climate of the country, the [[Tresden Empire]] was deemed uninhabitable by most of the populace. Most fleeing to neighbouring countries. Since this event Tresden is nothing but mostly barren land filled with ruins.
