Royal incinerators are a construct designed to be a deathless unstoppable soldier. Made possible by a hollow armour made of [[Uthalt]] capable of containing and channelling the animating and eternal magical flame held within the armour. A flame that is linked to another held in an [[Uthalt]] brazier keeping it animate.

Before [[The Great Burn]] it was believed royal incinerators were nothing more than a tale. Told as follows:

> "Centuries ago, king Norville ruled [[Tresden Empire]] with his mage and royal adviser Brenger by his side. At the Beginning of his rule Tresden prospered and he was loved by his people. Once [[Uthalt]] ran dry and the country began to spiral downwards, opinions of Norville changed in within certain groups. They believed that the king became complacent and that the loss of their countries pride, [[Uthalt]], could have been avoided.
> 
> Over the years stories of rebellion from these groups reached the king, they had developed a decent number of members and were beginning to build confidence to outwardly threaten King Norville. He, a prideful ruler, fearing an uprising wanted to squash these rebellions before they even began and after confiding in his adviser, Brenger devised a creation to do just that.
> 
> Born of the fusion between golems, made of the miracle metal [[Uthalt]], and the souls of heroes of legend, Royal Incinerators were threats to be unleashed as a last resort. The powerful soul animating them allows them to relentlessly charge into battle. The soul of the incinerator is held inside the golem, bound to a brazier. Once the brazier is lit, the incinerator awakens and can be given a singular task, a target or targets to kill. 
> 
> As long as the braziers flames burn, the incinerator will keep coming back to life, stronger and stronger, until the task is accomplished, at which point the incinerator will go dormant again. Once a mission is given, nothing can stop a royal incinerator, not even the person who gave the order. They are simply considered obstacles to be removed. Nothing is more important than the mission.
> 
> The Incinerators swiftly quelled the rebellious groups and peace was once again restored. Brenger however grew power hungry. He had come to align with the rebellions beliefs, seeing first hand Norvilles inaction even after Brenger had urged him to act on his belief that [[Uthalt]] was poached from under him. 
> 
> This new creation showed Brenger the power held in the flaming souls bound within the incinerator and inspired him to pursue it. Eventually, Brenger turned the Incinerators on Norville and Vanished never to be seen again."

It was believed that royal incinerators were actually just regular knights witnessed by bystanders during the battles against the rebellions on a burning battle field. the visions of the knight standing and fighting in flame twisted and exaggerated over time to become mindless flaming arbiters of death.

As with many legends and stories many did attempt to see if it was even possible to create a creation such as the incinerator. Despite many attempts was never recreated, leading many to agree that it is not possible and solidifying the story as just that, a story.

However with its appearance and activity during [[The Great Burn]] the tale became history. The royal Incinerator made again. Hunting a mercenary group for the [[Eternal Flame Cult]] ran by Brenger, the Incinerators original creator.

After a few battles with the Incinerator over about a week, the brazier inside the castle was extinguished and Brenger was defeated. It is believed that the [[Eternal Flame Cult]] was only able to scrounge up enough [[Uthalt]] to create one, and with the only known person with the magical knowledge and capability to animate the Royal Incinerator dead, it is likely that such a creation will never be remade.
